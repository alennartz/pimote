# Design Brief: Pimote Android App

> Generated by `ux-designer`. Push back on any decision in chat to update.

## At a glance

- **Surface:** The native Android client — three screens (Setup, Contacts/Sessions list, In-Call) plus the system-level in-call notification.
- **Audience:** Developers running `pi` coding agent on a home/work machine; they use this app to call their agent from a phone, in a car, or away from the keyboard. Technical, self-hosted, comfortable with Tailscale/VPN; not a general consumer.
- **Primary job-to-be-done:** Initiate and sustain a voice call to a `pi` session — from the Android dialer, the Android Auto contact picker, or directly inside the app — while staying in contact with the agent state even when eyes are not on the screen.
- **Target platforms:** Android phone (portrait primary), Android Auto (secondary, constraint-setting).
- **Hard constraints:** Minimum 48dp touch targets everywhere (Auto + driving use). Call screen must be usable eyes-off. No branding assets exist yet. The app does NOT render chat/scrollback — that belongs to the PWA.

---

## Direction

- **Track:** Utility
- **Dials:** Variance 3 / Density 5 / Motion 3 / Formality 4
- **One-line summary:** A calm, dark-native voice-calling tool in the tradition of Signal and professional VoIP apps, with developer-tool restraint.

**Why this direction.** The app's primary job is a voice call, not a feed or dashboard. The user's most demanding context is hands-free while driving — Motion stays low so the screen is a glance surface, not an animation display. Variance stays low because there are only three screens and visual surprise adds cognitive overhead. Formality sits below neutral because developers bristle at corporate polish, but the driving context demands the app feel reliable and composed, not playful.

---

## Reference set

- **Signal** — The calling-first VoIP UX: full-screen in-call surface, centered avatar circle with state pulse, large action buttons at the bottom, minimal chrome. We take the structural composition and the hierarchy (agent identity at the top → call state in the middle → actions at the bottom) and adapt it with developer-tool coloration instead of Signal's consumer blues.
- **Linear (mobile)** — The dark surface, the near-monochrome restraint, the use of a single accent on an otherwise achromatic field, the tiny-but-readable secondary text. We take the color DNA and the discipline of "one accent, not five," but we replace Linear's list density with more generous touch targets.
- **Aircall / Dialpad** — Professional VoIP calling: connection-status as a small persistent indicator (not a full-width banner), session names styled like contact names, reliable iconography over emoji. We take the "this is a phone call, not a toy" tone and the information hierarchy of the dialer contact list.

## Palette

All colors are specified for dark theme, which is the default and the mandatory context for Auto/driving use. A system-light mode variant is described at the end of this section but dark is primary.

### Dark theme (default)

**Foundation**

- **Void** `#0D0F14` — primary background — near-black with a blue undertone anchors the developer-tool mood and reads as "display screen" rather than "paper."
- **Surface** `#161922` — elevated surface (cards, bottom sheets, bottom action zone) — one step above Void without being obviously grey.
- **Surface+** `#1E222E` — tertiary surfaces, input fills, dividers-as-surface — creates a three-stop depth ladder so hierarchy is communicated by elevation alone.
- **Scrim** `rgba(0,0,0,0.6)` — modal overlays

**Text**

- **Ink** `#E4E8F2` — primary text — slightly cool white; avoids pure white glare on dark bg; comfortable for reading status strings.
- **Ink secondary** `#7D8699` — secondary text (subtitles, hints, call duration) — enough contrast for legibility without competing with primary.
- **Ink disabled** `#3D4252` — disabled / placeholder text

**Borders & dividers**

- **Line** `#252934` — subtle structural lines; not used between list items (whitespace does that job).

**Accent (one)**

- **Indigo** `#7B9FFF` — primary accent — interactive elements, call button ring, focus ring, active nav. Indigo reads as tech-forward and pairs cleanly with the blue-tinted dark bg. Passes WCAG AA (4.6:1 on Void).

**Semantic**

- **Active** `#4DC896` — teal-green — "Connected" status dot, active call timer, "call live" states. Warm-cool contrast with Indigo creates instant categorical distinction between "connected/alive" (teal) and "interactive/control" (indigo).
- **Warning** `#F0B34C` — amber — Reconnecting, degraded, retrying.
- **Danger** `#F26B6B` — soft red — Failed, call ended with error, hang-up button. Desaturated just enough to not scream; still unmistakably danger.
- **Idle** `#5A6070` — neutral grey — Disconnected state; "nothing happening" — flat grey avoids emotional signal.

**In-call avatar pulse colors** (the animated ring on the In-Call screen)

- Listening: Indigo `#7B9FFF` at 60% opacity outer, 100% inner
- Active / thinking: Indigo `#7B9FFF`, full opacity, slow pulse
- Ended: Danger `#F26B6B`, static

**Banned in this design:** No gradients on UI chrome. No glassmorphism. No neon/fluorescent accents (lime, electric blue, hot pink). No more than two accent colors active at once on any single screen. No light-theme-first color decisions reapplied to dark surfaces.

### Light theme (secondary, system-preference only)

If the device is in system light mode, use: Background `#F6F7FA`, Surface `#FFFFFF`, Surface+ `#EDEEF3`, Ink `#1A1D26`, Ink secondary `#6B7080`, Indigo accent `#3B65E8`, Active `#1E9E6B`, Warning `#C07800`, Danger `#D63E3E`. All other decisions remain identical.

## Typography

**Families**

- **UI (display, title, body, label):** `Inter` — weight range 400–600. Geometric, screen-optimised, the canonical developer-tool typeface. Falls back to `Roboto` on Android if Inter is not bundled (Roboto is already on-device; Inter is preferred and worth bundling as a variable font). No italic used anywhere.
- **Mono:** `JetBrains Mono` — weight 400 — used for URLs, session IDs, connection status strings, call duration counter. Already has developer-tool connotation; narrow glyphs help in status displays. Falls back to `Roboto Mono`.

**Scale.** Material 3 type scale as the structural skeleton, with the following overrides:

**Concrete sizes**

| Role                 | Size | Line-height | Weight | Tracking | Use                                                  |
| -------------------- | ---- | ----------- | ------ | -------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| Display / Call state | 32sp | 40sp        | 400    | −0.5px   | Large in-call state, agent name on call screen       |
| titleLarge           | 22sp | 28sp        | 600    | 0        | Screen titles in TopAppBar                           |
| titleMedium          | 18sp | 24sp        | 600    | 0        | Contact name (primary row text in contacts list)     |
| bodyLarge            | 16sp | 24sp        | 400    | 0        | Standard list body, setup instructions               |
| bodyMedium           | 14sp | 20sp        | 400    | 0        | Secondary list text, setup helper text               |
| bodySmall            | 12sp | 16sp        | 400    | 0        | Row subtitles ("Tap to call this session"), captions |
| labelMedium          | 12sp | 16sp        | 600    | 0.4px    | Status badge text, section labels                    |
| mono/14              | 14sp | 20sp        | 400    | 0        | URLs, connection status text, call duration          |
| mono/12              | 12sp | 16sp        | 400    | 0        | Session ID fragments in subtitles                    |

**Hierarchy strategy.** Size and weight carry most hierarchy — color plays a supporting role (Ink vs Ink secondary). Avoid relying on font color alone to distinguish levels.

**Banned in this design.** No system serif. No all-caps labels on interactive elements. No Title Case in body sentences. No font sizes below 11sp anywhere in the app.

## Spacing & rhythm

- **Base unit:** 8dp
- **Scale:** 4 / 8 / 12 / 16 / 20 / 24 / 32 / 48 / 64
- **Content padding:** 20dp horizontal on all screens (slightly wider than Material 3's 16dp default — more breathing room on phones being glanced at from a distance; works well in portrait).
- **Vertical rhythm:** 24dp between major sections; 16dp between related elements; 8dp between tightly-coupled pairs.
- **List item height:** 72dp minimum per row — ensures thumb-tappable in any grip, glove-friendly in Auto.
- **Action button height (In-Call):** 64dp minimum — the two call-action buttons need deliberate press, not accidental tap.
- **Concentric rule:** When a rounded container nests inside another, outer corner radius = inner corner radius + padding. (e.g., a 12dp-radius chip inside a 20dp-padded card card → card uses 32dp radius.)

## Components

### Status Pill (connection indicator)

The current "connection banner" (full-width row with a dot + monospace text) is replaced with a compact status pill anchored to the top of the Contacts screen, below the TopAppBar. It occupies minimal vertical space and auto-dismisses when `Connected` (collapses after 3 seconds to a 6dp dot-only indicator in the trailing space of the TopAppBar title row). When not Connected, it stays expanded.

**Anatomy.** A 6dp filled circle (state color) + label text (labelMedium, monospace) inside a rounded pill (corner radius 12dp) with 8dp vertical / 12dp horizontal padding. Background: Surface+. The pill sits at the top of the content area below the app bar, inset 20dp from both edges, aligned leading.

**Variants.**

- _Connected_ — Active `#4DC896` dot + "Connected" — collapses after 3s to a 6dp persistent dot in the app bar trailing area.
- _Connecting_ — Warning `#F0B34C` dot + "Connecting…"
- _Reconnecting_ — Warning dot + "Reconnecting · attempt N"
- _Failed_ — Danger dot + "Failed: [reason truncated to 40 chars]"
- _Disconnected_ — Idle dot + "Disconnected"

**States.**

- **Default:** Static pill, label visible.
- **Connected:** Cross-fades to collapsed dot in app bar trailing. If tapped while collapsed, expands back to full pill for 3s then collapses again.
- **Transitioning:** Cross-fades between states at 150ms / snappy.

**Behavior.** Not a navigation trigger. Tap on failed/disconnected state: scrolls to settings action in the app bar (scroll animation 200ms). Touch target: the full pill bounding box, minimum 48dp height via vertical padding.

**Content rules.** All text in lower sentence case. Reason string: strip "ws error:" prefix, truncate at 40 characters with "…" Never show raw stack traces.

### Contact Row

**Anatomy.** Full-width row containing: leading icon (24dp, tinted Indigo), title (titleMedium, Ink), subtitle (bodySmall, Ink secondary), trailing chevron icon (16dp, Ink secondary). No horizontal dividers between rows — 1dp × 80% width Line divider at the bottom of each row renders only if the next row exists and is a different type (projects then sessions). Row height: 72dp minimum.

**Leading icons.**

- Project → `folder_outlined` (Material icon). Folder rows are listed first, above sessions.
- Session → `chat_bubble_outlined` (Material icon). Not emoji — these are system icons with consistent weight.

**Subtitle copy.**

- Project row: "New session in this project"
- Session row: the session name if named; "Untitled session" if not. Preceded by a 10dp `call_outlined` icon (12dp) inline to signal this row places a call.

**States.**

- **Default:** Surface background, Ink text, Indigo icon.
- **Pressed:** Background flash to Surface+ at snappy speed (100ms), no persistent selection.
- **Loading (post-tap):** Leading icon replaced by 20dp CircularProgressIndicator in Indigo. Row remains in list — no skeleton replacement. Spinner auto-cleared if call fails (row returns to normal + snackbar error).
- **Empty list:** EmptyState component (see below).
- **Error:** Snackbar at bottom with Danger-tinted icon and actionable message.

**Behavior.** Tap → immediately triggers `placeCall`. Row is full-width clickable. Touch target: entire 72dp row height × screen width. No long-press actions in v1.

**Content rules.** Project labels: folder name only (no path). Session labels: max 60 characters before truncation with "…". Label case: preserve original (user-defined names).

### Empty State

Shown in the Contacts screen when the session list is empty. Context-sensitive based on WsState.

**Anatomy.** Full-screen centered column: a 48dp icon (outlined, Idle color), a 4dp gap, a bodyLarge primary message (Ink), an 8dp gap, a bodyMedium secondary message (Ink secondary), a 16dp gap, optional CTA button.

**States by ws-state:**

- **Connected, no sessions:** Icon: `dashboard_outlined`. Primary: "No sessions yet." Secondary: "Open a project in Pimote on the web — it will appear here as a contact." No CTA.
- **Connecting / Reconnecting:** Icon: `sync` (animate rotation at Snappy, continuous). Primary: "Connecting to Pimote." Secondary: "Your sessions will appear once the connection is established."
- **Failed:** Icon: `signal_wifi_bad`. Primary: "Couldn't connect." Secondary: "[reason]". CTA: "Open Settings" → navigates to Setup screen.
- **Disconnected:** Icon: `wifi_off`. Primary: "Not connected." Secondary: "Configure a server URL in Settings." CTA: "Open Settings".

**Behavior.** CTA button uses secondary variant (see Buttons). Icon animation only on Connecting/Reconnecting states.

### In-Call Screen

This is the hero screen. It should feel like a professional VoIP call surface — full-bleed, uncluttered, focused entirely on the call state and the two actions (mute, hang up).

**Anatomy — three vertical regions:**

1. **Header zone** (top, ~20% of screen height): Session name in titleLarge (Ink), project name in bodyMedium (Ink secondary) below it, both center-aligned. TopAppBar is not shown on this screen — the header zone replaces it. No navigation affordance while a call is active; after the call ends, a "←" back button appears leading in the header.

2. **Avatar zone** (middle, ~45% of screen height): A centered 120dp circle. Fill: Surface+. Stroke: 2dp ring in Indigo (call active), Danger (call ended with error), Idle (connecting states). The ring animates based on agent state:
   - **Dialing / Binding / Negotiating:** A dashed-stroke arc rotates around the circle (smooth infinite rotation, 1200ms/cycle, Standard timing). Label below the circle: call phase in labelMedium (Indigo), e.g., "Dialing…", "Connecting…".
   - **Active:** The ring becomes a solid radial pulse — scale animates 1.0 → 1.06 → 1.0 on a 2400ms loop, opacity 1.0 → 0.65 → 1.0. This indicates "live." Below the circle: a monospace call duration timer (mono/14, Active color), updating every second.
   - **Ended (success):** Ring static, Idle color. Label: "Call ended" in labelMedium. No pulse.
   - **Ended (error):** Ring static, Danger color. Below: reason string in bodySmall (Danger), e.g., "Voice peer failed."
     Inside the circle: the first letter of the session/project name as a Display text in Ink secondary — an abstract "avatar." No photos.

3. **Action zone** (bottom, ~35% of screen height): Two large action buttons, horizontally centered with 24dp between them. Both are circular with 64dp diameter (minimum touch target). The zone has 24dp top padding and sits on Surface.
   - **Mute button:** Left button. Icon `mic_outlined` (unmuted) or `mic_off` (muted). Background: Surface+ (unmuted) or Warning `#F0B34C` (muted). Icon: Ink. Label below: "Mute" / "Unmute" in bodySmall (Ink secondary).
   - **Hang up button:** Right button. Icon `call_end`. Background: Danger `#F26B6B`. Icon: `#FFFFFF`. Label below: "End call" in bodySmall (Ink secondary). Slightly larger than Mute button (72dp) to reduce accidental mute/hangup confusion.
   - When call is Ended: both action buttons replaced by a single "Close" button (secondary variant, bodyLarge label "Close") centered in the action zone.

**States.**

- **Dialing / Connecting:** Spinner ring, no timer, no action buttons yet except Hang Up (allows aborting a dial in progress). Mute is disabled (greyed out) until Active.
- **Active:** Full UI live. Mute toggles immediately.
- **Ended:** Both primary action buttons replaced by "Close." Duration counter freezes. Ring static.
- **Muted:** Mute button background changes to Warning. A `mic_off` badge (12dp, Warning) appears below the avatar circle, inside the avatar zone.

**Behavior.** Back gesture (Android system) is disabled while a call is Active — user must use "End call" button. After Ended state, back gesture and "Close" button both dismiss. Screen stays on (`FLAG_KEEP_SCREEN_ON`) while Active.

**Content rules.** Session name: max 48 characters before truncation. Phase labels: lower sentence case, verb-forward ("Dialing…", "Connecting…", "Active"). End reason: one brief phrase, no internal error codes shown.

### Buttons

**Anatomy.** Rounded rectangle (corner radius 12dp), label in labelMedium (600 weight), height 52dp (comfortable touch target), horizontal padding 24dp.

**Variants.**

- _Primary_ — Background: Indigo `#7B9FFF`. Label: `#000000` (black on indigo, ensures contrast). Used for: "Connect" on Setup, any one primary CTA per screen.
- _Secondary_ — Background: Surface+. Label: Ink. Stroke: 1dp Line. Used for: "Continue to contacts," "Close," settings actions.
- _Destructive_ — Background: Danger `#F26B6B`. Label: `#FFFFFF`. Used only in confirmation dialogs if any appear in future.
- _Ghost_ — Transparent background, no stroke, Label: Indigo. Used for: "Skip" or low-priority secondary actions.

**States.**

- **Default:** As described per variant.
- **Hover (pointer):** 8% Ink overlay on background.
- **Focus-visible:** 2dp Indigo ring, 2dp gap from button edge.
- **Active (pressed):** 16% Ink overlay, scale 0.98 at Snappy timing.
- **Disabled:** Opacity 0.38. No interaction.
- **Loading:** Leading CircularProgressIndicator (16dp, variant-matching color) + label "Connecting…" or similar. Width unchanged (don't jump).

**Content rules.** Sentence case. Verb-first: "Connect", "Hang up", "Close", not "OK", "Submit", "Continue" (unless "Continue" is literally the action). Max 24 characters per label.

### Outlined Text Field (Setup URL input)

**Anatomy.** Material 3 OutlinedTextField with Inter body, 52dp height, 12dp corner radius. Label floats above on focus.

**States.**

- **Default (empty):** Line border in Line color. Placeholder: "https://pimote.example.com" in Ink disabled.
- **Focused:** 2dp Indigo border. Floating label: "Pimote server URL" in Indigo.
- **Filled:** 1dp Line border. Floating label: Ink secondary.
- **Error:** 2dp Danger border. Floating label: Danger. Helper text below: error message in bodySmall (Danger).
- **Disabled:** Opacity 0.38.

**Content rules.** Float label: "Pimote server URL". Placeholder: a real-looking HTTPS URL. Helper text on error: actionable ("URL must start with https:// or http://", not "invalid URL").

### Snackbar

**Anatomy.** Floating pill at the bottom of the screen, 16dp margin from screen edges, 12dp corner radius, 52dp height, Surface+ background with 1dp Line stroke. Leading icon (16dp, semantic color) + bodyMedium text (Ink) + optional trailing "Dismiss" in Indigo (ghost style). Auto-dismisses after 4s.

**Variants.**

- _Error:_ Danger icon `error_outline`. Used for connection failures, call failures.
- _Info:_ No icon. Used for neutral informational messages.

### TopAppBar

**Anatomy.** Standard Material 3 TopAppBar, Surface+ background (no shadow/elevation), title in titleLarge (Inter 600, Ink), leading back arrow (Ink) when navigation is possible, trailing icon buttons (24dp icons, Ink).

**Content rules.** Title: the screen name, sentence case. Screens: "Pimote" (Contacts), "Settings" (Setup). Never truncate the title to show a subtitle.

## Composition & layout

- **First-impression rule.** Opening the app lands on the Contacts screen with the status pill prominent: the user's first question is always "am I connected?" — answer it immediately, above the fold.
- **Layout strategy.** Single column, full-bleed. No sidebars, no cards within cards. The list is edge-to-edge content. The In-Call screen is compositionally a portrait poster: three vertical bands (header / avatar / actions) with deliberate breathing room around the avatar circle.
- **Contacts screen.** TopAppBar (title "Pimote" + Refresh + Settings icons) → status pill → list. Status pill is always the first element after the app bar — its position is locked, not scrollable.
- **Setup screen.** TopAppBar ("Settings") → 20dp vertical padding → instruction text → URL field → Connect button → connection status block → if already connected: "Continue" button. The vertical flow is a single column; no side-by-side elements at phone width.
- **In-Call screen.** No TopAppBar. Header zone flush to status bar. Avatar zone vertically centered in remaining space. Action zone pinned to bottom. The three zones maintain their proportions on all phone form factors via flexible height allocation (not fixed px values).
- **Hard rules.** No bottom navigation bar (three screens don't need it; they're linear). No FAB on Contacts or Setup. No cards wrapping individual list rows (row IS the surface). No empty space filled with decorative illustration.

## Responsive

- **Phone portrait (primary):** As specified throughout. 360–430dp width range assumed.
- **Phone landscape:** In-Call screen rotates gracefully — header zone narrows, avatar shifts left with action zone right, using a two-column layout (header+avatar left, actions right) when width > 600dp.
- **Android Auto:** Not a layout the app renders directly — Auto renders through the Telecom system UI. The app's design ensures registered contact names are clean and scannable (≤ 40 characters, no path prefixes) for the Auto contact picker.
- **Touch targets.** Minimum 48dp × 48dp on all interactive elements. Action zone buttons are 64dp+ — never below 48dp.
- **Type scaling.** No dynamic type scaling in v1. Fixed sp values as specified. Revisit for accessibility follow-up.
- **Navigation transformation.** No transformation — the app is phone-only in its rendered surfaces. Navigation is screen push/pop (no tabs, no drawer).

## Motion intent

**Motion philosophy.** Motion communicates state, not personality. Every animation on this surface has one job: tell the user what changed or what the call is doing. Nothing animates for decoration. The driving/eyes-off constraint means animation must be subtle enough to not distract when glanced at, and the three-state live indicator on the in-call screen is the only ambient motion.

**Timing classes.**

- _Snappy_ (100–150ms) — button press feedback, mute state toggle, row press.
- _Standard_ (200–250ms) — screen push/pop transitions, status pill expand/collapse, snackbar appear/dismiss.
- _Expressive_ (not used in v1 — no marketing reveals, no modals beyond snackbar).

**Where animation appears.**

- Button press: Snappy scale + overlay.
- Screen push (e.g., tap Settings from Contacts): Standard slide-in from right. Screen pop: Standard slide-out to right.
- Status pill state change (e.g., Connecting → Connected): Standard cross-fade between text+color.
- Status pill collapse (Connected → dot): Standard fade-out of text, dot persists.
- In-call ring pulse (Active state): 2400ms loop, ease-in-out scale + opacity. Ambient, not snappy.
- In-call connecting arc: 1200ms infinite rotation, linear easing.
- Mute button background toggle: Snappy cross-fade between Surface+ and Warning.
- Snackbar entrance: Standard slide-up from bottom. Snackbar exit: Standard fade-out.

**Continuity rules.**

- Status pill emerges from its position, not from the top edge.
- Screens slide directionally (right = forward, left = back) — no cross-fade-only transitions.
- The in-call pulse begins as soon as `CallState.Active` is observed — no delay.
- Exit animations are 20% faster than entrances.
- Reduced motion (`prefers-reduced-motion` / Android's "Remove animations"): all animation replaced with instant state changes, including the ring pulse (which becomes a static ring) and the connecting arc (which becomes a static partial arc).

**What does not animate.** List rows (no stagger-in, no individual row fade). Icon state changes within a button (swap is instant). Text content changes (instant). The avatar circle initial render. TopAppBar shadow (there is no shadow).

## Content & copy

- **Voice.** Utility — direct, factual, no personality performance. The app is a tool; the voice matches.
- **Screen titles.** One word or two. Sentence case. "Pimote" (main), "Settings" (setup screen). Never "Pimote — Your AI Agent Phone."
- **Status strings.** Present-tense, lowercase, no punctuation except trailing "…" for in-progress states: "Connected", "Connecting…", "Reconnecting · attempt 2", "Failed: [reason]", "Disconnected". These use monospace type.
- **Row subtitles.** Lowercase verb phrases: "New session in this project", "Tap to call this session". Short, literal.
- **Empty states.** Two sentences max. First: state the situation. Second: say what to do: "No sessions yet. Open a project in Pimote on the web — it'll appear here." Never commiserate.
- **Errors.** Name the problem, give an action: "Couldn't connect. Check the URL in Settings, or verify your VPN is active." Not: "Oops! Something went wrong."
- **In-call labels.** Verb+ellipsis for in-progress: "Dialing…", "Connecting…", "Binding…". Adjective for settled states: "Active", "Call ended", "Muted". End reason: short noun phrase, no jargon: "Session not found", "Voice peer failed", "Hung up from another device."
- **Button labels.** Imperative verb or verb phrase: "Connect", "Hang up", "End call", "Mute", "Unmute", "Close", "Open Settings", "Refresh". Never "OK", "Yes", "Submit", "Proceed".

**Banned phrases.** "Oops!", "Something went wrong", "We couldn't", "Let's get started", "All good!", "Seamless", "Powerful", exclamation marks on success states, "Please try again later" (say what to check instead), Lorem Ipsum.

## Accessibility

- **Contrast.** WCAG AA minimum. Ink `#E4E8F2` on Void `#0D0F14` = 14.2:1 (exceeds AAA). Indigo `#7B9FFF` on Void = 4.7:1 (AA body). Danger `#F26B6B` on Void = 4.5:1 (AA). Active `#4DC896` on Void = 5.1:1 (AA). All UI text meets 4.5:1; large text (22sp+) and non-text UI elements meet 3:1.
- **Hit targets.** Minimum 48dp × 48dp on all interactive elements. In-call action buttons minimum 64dp diameter. Status pill minimum 48dp height via padding.
- **Focus visibility.** Every interactive element: 2dp Indigo ring, 2dp gap from element edge, visible on all backgrounds in this palette.
- **Color is never the sole carrier of meaning.** Status pill: color + text label + icon. Mute state: color + icon swap (`mic` / `mic_off`) + label change. Call state: color + text label. Connection states: color + text label.
- **Screen reader.** Contact rows: contentDescription = "[session/project name], [subtitle]". Status pill: contentDescription updated on state change, announced as LiveRegion (ARIA polite equivalent). In-call avatar: contentDescription = "Agent [name], [call state]", updated on state transitions. Mute button: contentDescription = "Mute [or Unmute] microphone". Hang up: contentDescription = "End call".
- **Reduced motion.** All animations (pulse, arc rotation, transitions) replaced with instant state changes when "Remove animations" is enabled in Developer Options.
- **Keyboard/external.** Tab order: top to bottom, left to right. Enter/Space activates buttons. No focus traps outside of the active call screen (where back gesture is intentionally disabled).

## Anti-patterns

- **No hardcoded hex color strings in component code.** All palette references go through the theme token system.
- **No emoji as functional icons.** 📁 and 🗨 in the current code are placeholders — replace with Material outlined icons (`folder_outlined`, `chat_bubble_outlined`).
- **No full-width banner for a connection status that's usually "Connected".** The current full-row ConnectionBanner is disproportionate attention for what is usually a non-issue. Use the pill that collapses.
- **No plain `Text("Pimote call")` as a call screen heading.** The current in-call screen has no visual hierarchy — it's a vertical list of strings and two buttons. The In-Call screen needs to feel like a phone call, not a debug log.
- **No auto-dismiss of Ended call state.** The current code shows "Reason: X" — the design preserves this. Don't auto-finish the activity on Ended; the user must tap Close.
- **No animation "sprinkle."** Motion is enumerated above. Anything not in the list does not animate.
- **No more than two visible accent colors at once on any screen.** Indigo for interactive + one semantic (Active, Warning, or Danger depending on state).
- **No truncation of session names without "…".** Always append ellipsis when clipping, never hard-chop.
- **No toast-only error reporting for the setup flow.** If connection fails after a "Connect" attempt, surface the error in-line below the button (not just a snackbar that the user may miss). Snackbar is supplemental.
- **No elevation shadow on the TopAppBar.** Flat surface — shadows on a near-black background are invisible noise.
- **No persistent bottom navigation bar.** Three linear screens do not need it.
- **No placeholder "coming soon" UI.** If voice transcription or session chat is not in scope for this native app, don't show a greyed-out tab or a locked feature. Omit it entirely.

## Open questions

- **App icon / launcher icon:** No brand assets exist yet. The brief assumes a monogram "P" or an abstract waveform on an Indigo circle as a placeholder. Once a brand identity is established, the icon should be revisited. Not a blocker for the UI.
- **Incoming calls (FCM-triggered):** The brainstorm (voice-mode-android.md) scopes agent-initiated calls for a future phase. The In-Call screen design above covers the outgoing-call-initiated UI; the incoming call notification uses Android's `CallStyle` notification which is system-rendered. No new design decisions needed for incoming calls in v1, but the color/name treatment in `CallStyle` notifications should match this palette (caller name in Ink, action buttons using system call colors, not custom).
- **Dark/light mode toggle:** This brief specifies dark as default and provides a light palette translation. The question of whether to expose a manual in-app toggle (vs. always following system preference) is a product decision, not a design one. Recommendation: follow system preference only; no in-app toggle.
